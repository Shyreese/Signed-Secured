An apostille is a specialized certificate from the Secretary of State that authenticates documents for use in foreign countries. We help you navigate this complex process.

What to Provide

  • ✓ The original document needing authentication.
  • ✓ The name of the destination country.

How It Works

  1. Verification: We first verify your document is eligible and notarize it if required.
  2. State Submission: We then present your documents to the California Secretary of State for the final apostille certificate.
  3. Final Return: Once authenticated, we return the completed documents to you, ready for international use.

Common documents we apostille include birth/death certificates, marriage licenses, powers of attorney, school transcripts, corporate documents, and more.

How does the mobile notary process work?

It's simple. First, you book an appointment online. We then travel to your preferred location in the Greater Sacramento area. At the appointment, we verify the signer's identity with a valid photo ID, witness the signing, and complete the notarization. It’s a secure and convenient service from start to finish.

How are fees determined for a mobile notary?

For transparency, our pricing is simple: a standard, state-regulated fee for each signature notarized, plus a mobile convenience fee for travel from our base in Rancho Cordova. We will verify the signature count and provide a final quote for your approval before we begin.

What do I need for my appointment?

Please have two things ready: the complete, unsigned document(s) you need notarized, and a valid, government-issued photo ID for every person signing. Acceptable forms of ID include a state driver's license, passport, or military ID.
